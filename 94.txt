He was, however, the community’s most widely known citizen, prominent both there and inGarden City, the close-by county seat, where he had headed the building committee for the newly completed First Methodist Church, an eight hundred-thousand-dollar edifice. He was currently chairman of the Kansas Conference of Farm Organizations, and his name was everywhere respectfully recognized among Midwestern agriculturists, as it was in certain Washington offices, where he had been a member of the Federal Farm Credit Board during the Eisenhower administration.
